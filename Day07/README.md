# Day 07 – Week 1 Reflection

## Which task helped you learn the most and why?
The phishing analysis task helped me the most. I felt like I was thinking like an analyst for the first time. Instead of just reading the email, I was questioning everything — the link, the domain, even the psychology behind it. It made me more aware of how small details can reveal a bigger threat.

## One concept that became clearer for you this week.
The idea that not all attacks are technical. Some attacks are purely psychological. This week made me realize how powerful social engineering is and how easily people can be manipulated without any complex hacking involved.

## One concept you are still confused about.
I still feel like I need a deeper understanding of how email authentication actually works behind the scenes. I understand the purpose, but I want to know the technical flow step by step so I can fully connect the concept.

## One mistake you made and what you learned from it.
I sometimes overthink my answers and try to make them sound too professional. I realized that focusing on understanding the concept clearly is more important than trying to make everything sound perfect.

## What habit helped you stay consistent?
I treated the tasks like a commitment, not something optional. Even if I was tired, I tried to finish the task the same day instead of postponing it. That helped me build discipline.

## What will you do differently in Week 2?
I want to focus more on practical understanding, not just completing the task. I also plan to review previous concepts briefly before starting a new task so I can connect ideas better.

## What topic would you like to learn next in cybersecurity?
I would love to explore log analysis and understand how SOC analysts detect suspicious behavior from real logs. I’m especially curious about how real incident investigations are handled step by step.

## Any suggestions or feedback for improving this internship program?
The structure is clear and helpful so far. Maybe adding more small real-life scenarios or optional challenge tasks would make it even more engaging and practical.
