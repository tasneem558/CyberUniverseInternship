# Day 18 – Teach One Concept

## 1. The concept I chose

I chose to explain the **Firewall**.


## 2. My simple explanation

I think of a firewall like a **security guard** standing at the entrance of a wedding or an event.

The security guard checks if people are allowed to enter or not.

- If you have the invitation card, you can enter.
- If you are a stranger or you don’t have permission, you are blocked.

This is similar to how a firewall works. It checks incoming or outgoing traffic and decides what is allowed and what should be blocked.

But the firewall is not perfect. Sometimes, a bad or suspicious thing can still pass if it looks trusted or comes with something that already has permission.

That’s why a firewall is important, but it is not enough by itself.


## 3. One part that was difficult to explain

The hardest part to explain was that a firewall does **not block everything**.

Many beginners may think it stops all threats, but some harmful things can still get through if they look normal or trusted.


## 4. One example or analogy I used

I used the **security guard at a wedding or event** example.

It helped me explain how the firewall checks who is allowed to pass and who should be blocked.
